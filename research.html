Research themes

Our research aims to understand the theoretical and mathematical interplay of information, computational and physical foundations. We develop applied protocols that utilise foundational properties such as locality, entanglement and causality. Our algorithms aim to efficiently solve new problems and learn from unknown quantum oracles — for implementation in quantum communication and experimental realisation.

Quantum
Algorithmics

We design algorithms that harness quantum superposition, interference, and entanglement to solve computational problems beyond the reach of any classical method. Our work spans query complexity, quantum walks, algebraic techniques, and novel paradigms for search, simulation, and sampling — with a focus on proving tight upper and lower bounds on quantum query and time complexity.

Refs arXiv:0811.3171 arXiv:quant-ph/0311001

Quantum Information
Theory

How do quantum systems store, process, and communicate information? We study entanglement structure, quantum channel capacities, entropy measures, and quantum error-correcting codes. Our results build the information-theoretic foundations that underpin quantum computing and connect to physics, mathematics, and communication theory.

Refs arXiv:1907.11157 arXiv:quant-ph/9604038
03 / 06

Complexity Theory &
Quantum Complexity Theory

We characterise the power and limits of quantum computation through the lens of complexity theory — studying complexity classes such as BQP, QMA, QIP, and QPCP, and mapping their relationships to classical counterparts. Our proofs draw on algebraic geometry, interactive proof systems, and novel reductions to establish where quantum computers are provably more powerful.

Refs arXiv:2001.04383 arXiv:quant-ph/0406180
04 / 06

Cryptography

We build cryptographic protocols whose security is guaranteed by the laws of quantum mechanics, not computational hardness assumptions. From quantum key distribution and device-independent protocols to post-quantum constructions secure against quantum adversaries, our work establishes the cryptographic foundations needed for the quantum era.

Refs arXiv:quant-ph/0003004 arXiv:0709.4267
05 / 06

Higher-Order Maps
& Causality

We study quantum processes that go beyond ordinary quantum circuits — higher-order maps that transform quantum channels, and causal structures that may themselves be in quantum superposition. This includes the quantum switch, process matrices, and indefinite causal order, with applications to quantum communication advantages, computational speedups, and the foundations of quantum theory.

Refs arXiv:0912.0195 arXiv:1406.5133
06 / 06

Graph Theory
& Networks

Graphs are the universal language of structure. We study graph-theoretic problems in both classical and quantum settings — from spectral graph theory and graph isomorphism to quantum walks on graphs and network-based quantum protocols. Our work develops new algorithms, hardness results, and structural insights for fundamental combinatorial problems on graphs and hypergraphs.

Refs arXiv:quant-ph/0012090 arXiv:1310.3575
